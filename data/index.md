Behind every business question is data. I focus on turning that data into answers people can actually use.

Most of my work centers on building practical, reliable reporting that helps teams understand what’s happening, why it’s happening, and where to focus next. I enjoy working with messy or incomplete data and turning it into something clean, structured, and actually usable.

I primarily work with Power BI, SQL, and data modeling to design scalable reporting solutions. That includes building semantic models, shaping data for usability, and creating dashboards that support self-service without sacrificing accuracy or trust.

My approach comes down to a few principles:

- Reporting should reduce dependency, not create it
- Metrics should be clearly defined and consistently applied
- Dashboards should answer real questions, not just display data
- Performance and maintainability matter just as much as visuals

Across my work, I focus on:

- Self-service reporting that minimizes manual effort
- KPI standardization and governance
- Identifying trends, risks, and performance gaps early
- Supporting cross-functional teams with shared, reliable data

This portfolio is a mix of structured projects and working examples—some are fully developed analyses, while others capture specific patterns, techniques, or solutions I’ve built along the way.

Together, they reflect how I approach data problems: starting with messy or incomplete inputs, structuring them into something usable, and building reporting that is clear, scalable, and reliable.
